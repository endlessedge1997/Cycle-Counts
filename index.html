📏 Diameter Method

Measure outer roll diameter, the inner core diameter, and the material thickness.

L = π × (OD² − ID²) / (4 × t)

⚖ Weight Method

Weigh the full roll, then the empty core (or skip if unknown). Pick a saved material to auto-fill yield, or enter it manually.

L = (W_gross − W_core) / W_per_length

⚙ Calibrate New Material

Don't know the manufacturer's yield? Take a roll where you know the length (a full new roll spec, or use the diameter method above), weigh it, and save the calculated yield to your library. After this, you only need a scale for that material.

👤 FOR THE COUNTER (Warehouse)
  1. Tap the CC pill at top → pick the cycle count you're counting (or use the chooser that pops up when the app opens).
  2. Go to 📋 Count tab. Scan a barcode (or tap a row). The matching item card opens.
  3. If the item has one row, the keypad opens automatically. If multiple rows, tap the one you're at.
  4. Type the quantity on the keypad, tap ✔ LOG.
  5. Continue until all rows are counted. The header pill shows X/Y progress.
  6. When done, go to 📤 Export tab → tap Copy Current CSV → open your email app, paste it into a message to the manager, and send.

👔 FOR THE MANAGER

Run this any time Pace inventory changes. Two steps: scrape Pace → upload to the gist. All warehouse phones see the new data on next open (or via Sync Now).

STEP 1 — Scrape Pace on a computer:
  1. Open any Pace page, logged in.
  2. Press F12, click Console.
  3. Tap the button below to copy the script.
  4. Paste in the Pace console, press Enter.
  5. A combined CSV (all 5 CCs) downloads to your computer.
STEP 2 — Upload that CSV to the gist:
  1. Tap the button below.
  2. Pick the CSV you just downloaded (likely in your Downloads folder).
  3. First time only: a prompt asks for your GitHub Personal Access Token. Paste it. Saved here only.
  4. Wait for "✓ Uploaded + synced…" status.

After the counter copies their counts and emails (pastes) them to you, push the values into the matching Pace cycle count.

  1. Open Pace in a desktop browser. Navigate to the same CC's detail page as the counter's CSV (e.g. CC 5085).
  2. Press F12, click Console.
  3. Tap the button below to copy the Fill script.
  4. Paste in the Pace console, press Enter. A white overlay dialog appears.
  5. Open the counter's email → select the CSV block (from "Cycle Count","Item Number",… through the last row) → copy.
  6. Paste into the dialog's text area, click Fill Counts.
  7. Review the filled values in Pace. Click Save.
